What's a Churro?

Churro recipe link

Description

A churro is a delightful, deep-fried pastry that is crispy on the outside and soft on the inside. Originating from Spain and popularized in many countries, churros are made from a simple dough of flour, water, butter, and a pinch of salt. The dough is piped through a star-shaped nozzle into hot oil, creating its signature ridged texture. Once golden and crispy, the churros are typically rolled in a cinnamon-sugar mixture, adding a sweet and aromatic finish.

Churros are often enjoyed with a dipping sauce, such as rich chocolate or dulce de leche, making them a perfect treat for any time of the day. They are a favorite at fairs, carnivals, and breakfast tables alike. Homemade churros are relatively easy to prepare and offer a warm, comforting indulgence that is sure to delight both young and old. With their simple ingredients and satisfying texture, churros are a timeless treat that brings joy with every bite.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 4-6 cups vegetable oil for frying
  • 1 egg
  • Caramel and/or chocolate sauce
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 tablespoon cinnamon
  • 5 tablespoons salted butter

Steps

  1. Boil 1 cup of water with 2 tablespoons of butter and a pinch of salt.
  2. Add 1 cup of flour and stir until the dough forms a ball.
  3. Let the dough cool slightly, then add 2 beaten eggs and mix well.
  4. Heat oil in a deep pan to 375°F (190°C).
  5. Pipe the dough through a star-shaped nozzle into the hot oil.
  6. Fry until golden brown, then drain on paper towels.
  7. Roll the churros in a cinnamon-sugar mixture.
  8. Serve with chocolate or dulce de leche dipping sauce.
